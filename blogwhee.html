Making of Whee !! - Worlds 1st Codable Sound Toy

Is it possible to teach Coding with Sounds & Images? 


The Big Idea!


For last couple of years we have been toying with the idea of a "Sound Maker Kit" for children that could also teach them coding. The idea hit us when we saw toddlers playing with toys. All children are inherently creative with sounds and music. A toddler‚Äôs playtime is filled with ‚ÄúVROOOMMS VROOOMs‚Äù of cars and ‚ÄúCLINK CLING CLINGs‚Äù of fairy‚Äôs wand. Music Instruments are an obvious next for children to continue their creativity with sounds but most instruments will take years to master and often they are complicated for a kid to learn and operate. Full scale instruments are often made keeping adults in mind.


Is it possible to think of a simple Sound Maker Kit which is easy enough for a 4 year old but sophisticated enough for kids to grow with? The very concept of "Whee !!" was born from this thought.

Development


The objectives were simple.


  1. Working should be as easy as it can get.
  2. The toy should be very versatile. Should support endless creativity and only limited by imagination.
  3. Should be attractive for a child. Must be gender neutral.

From Idea to Product:

The hardware design was pretty straight forward. No drama here. It should satisfy objectives 1 and 3 we hope.


As for the 2nd objective (support for unlimited imagination) we went for the tried and tested choice: A codable software! But how can we make it easy enough for a 4 year old to code? Is it even possible for a 4 year old to code? Then came our Eureka moment!

For coding, Kids should be at least 6 years old because they need to read in order to code. Can we code without words? or even better, Can we code with Images and Sounds? Whee Blocks is the answer to all these questions. It is the 2nd simplest form of coding. (the 1st is Draw-Me. We will get in to that later). All kids need is to code their sounds in Whee Blocks software (shown above) and upload it to Whee !!

Whee Blocks software also comes loaded with a pack of 50 ready to mix rhymes.
