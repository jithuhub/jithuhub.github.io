Story of 'Draw-Me'​ - The Simplest Coding Toy

What could be the Simplest form of Coding?


We started playing around with this idea during the last leg of development of Whee !!.﻿ At the very root of Coding is the idea of Computational Thinking conceived by Prof Mitchel Resnick and Prof Karen Brennan of MIT Media Labs. 

Cuny, Snyder, and Wing (2010) define computational thinking as “The thought processes involved in formulating problems and their solutions so that the solutions are represented in a form that can be effectively carried out by an information-processing agent”

The Idea:


We started asking: Is it possible to extend the concepts of Computational Thinking so that even a toddler can understand and code? Can we code without computers or Screens? From these questions and after months of brainstorming Draw-Me was born. Draw-Me is the simplest Coding Toy yet.With Draw-Me, children can scribble their code. All they need is a crayon and a wall.


The Concept:


Draw-Me has 3 parts:


  1. A friendly wooden Robot
  2. Two Maps - One big and One small ( of a city, solar system, world etc)
  3. Sensor blocks which can be magnetically snapped to crayons or marker pens


To Play with Draw-Me, kids need to follow these 3 simple steps!

Connect the Magnetic Blocks

Draw the Code

Ta-Da

Hows this coding?


Lets consider a possible scenario while playing with Draw-Me. The problem statement for a child could be something like " Help Draw-Me buy some clothes for Princess in the Castle". For solving this problem The child will need to route Draw-Me to the boutique and then to the Castle. This problem solving thought process could be the first training for a child towards Computational Thinking. The scribbling She/He will do to route Draw-me could be a child's very first Program. Once a child brain picks up this problem solving approach, She/He can soon scribble more complex algorithms.


We hope you have enjoyed the concept of Draw-Me. Do share your thoughts and ideas.
